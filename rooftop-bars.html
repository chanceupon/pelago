Rooftop Bars
Suggested Duration:
Up to 3 hours
If rooftop bars were the Oscars, then Singapore would be that A-list celebrity sweeping home all major categories. From glammed-up lounges towering several stories high to charming low-lying establishments with scenic views, Singapore’s nightlife is bursting at the seams with all sorts of rooftop bars ready to take your breath away.

Highlights
  • CÉ LA VI, the big boy of Singapore’s rooftop bar scene and luxurious space located on the 57th floor of Marina Bay Sands
  • Dusk Restaurant & Bar, great for romantic dates and a glorious sunset view
  • LeVeL33 is a penthouse restaurant and lounge with the world’s highest microbrewery
Singapore’s nightlife DNA isn’t complete without rooftop bars. In a space-constrained city, it makes sense for nightlife venues to be located high in the sky. Also, there’s nothing better than knocking back a tipple or two with an unblemished view and an open-air breeze, the latter being the perfect tonic for Singapore’s humidity.
If you love the high-roller life, head to CÉ LA VI. Fine-dining restaurant, sky bar, and club lounge rolled into one, this luxurious institution is the piece de resistance of Marina Bay Sands, perched 57 storeys high with an unparalleled view of the buzzing Marina Bay district. Indulge in modern Asian cuisine before heading to the sky bar for cocktails. End your night with a pulsating dance session at the electric club lounge.
For a whole lot more passion and romance, there’s Dusk Restaurant & Bar, an off-grid rooftop bar. Sitting 100-metres above sea level and surrounded by lush rainforest greenery, this romantic spot has a splendid vantage point for catching the sunset, with flawless sea views of Sentosa and Harbourfront.
Beer geeks, hit up LeVeL33. With its striking locale at the roof of the Marina Bay Financial Centre, here’s where you can guzzle down craft beers brewed in-house. Call in and try landing a seat at the outdoor terrace, but do so early as spots are limited.
A little more low-slung in terms of height is Smoke & Mirrors, perched on the 6th floor of the National Gallery Singapore. With fancy equipment like a rotary evaporator that helps to create unique flavours for cocktails, this classy outfit is one of the best places to hit up for a drink in the city. Plus, you’ll get a stunning panoramic view of the Marina Bay district.
More rooftop garden than a rooftop bar, Mr. Stork at Andaz makes for an intimate escape from the crowds. Inspired by storks that build nests in trees, the 39-storey high bar has 10 teepee huts for you to unwind in, with delicious botanical cocktails the perfect complement to a picturesque sky-high view that’ll rival any other.
Rounding things off is Potato Head Singapore. Housed in a heritage building in Chinatown, this multi-storey establishment serves up juicy burgers and tropical cocktails, with Balearic beats blasting in the background. Head to the rooftop area for a beautiful view of the Central Business District (CBD).
Insider Tips
  • CÉ LA VI has an incredible Ladies’ Night offer. On Wednesdays from 10pm till late, ladies get a glass of bubbly and free entry.
