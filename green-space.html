Green Spaces
Suggested Duration:
Up to 4 hours
Singapore may be a sprawling metropolis, but it has more than its fair share of green spaces. Some three million trees cover the city, with virgin rainforests and picturesque city parks with interlinked cycling trails. Coupled with vertical gardens and urban farms, it’s no surprise Singapore lives up to its reputation as a clean and green ‘Garden City’.

Highlights
  • Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve is Singapore’s first ASEAN Heritage Park, home to mangroves, mudflats, ponds, and migratory birds
  • MacRitchie Reservoir Park, an idyllic spot for hiking or kayaking, with a treetop walk high above the forest canopy
  • The 10-km trail called Southern Ridges connects popular city parks with stunning pedestrian bridges
Singapore loves branding itself as the Garden City. That utopian vision of a city surrounded by lush greenery was introduced in 1967 by then Prime Minister Lee Kuan Yew. An intensive, almost obsessive, tree-planting programme was kickstarted by the Parks and Trees Division, the predecessor of today’s National Parks Board (NParks).
The result? Tree-lined boulevards are found everywhere, with neatly trimmed lawns and community gardens in HDB estates (aka the heartlands ). Beyond that, you can visit a wide variety of parks for a much-needed escape from the bustling city, with some green spaces even boasting a rich biodiversity of flora and fauna.
Today, hawker centres can be found all over Singapore, from the Central Business District (CBD) to far-flung heartland areas.
For instance, Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve, Singapore’s first ASEAN Heritage Park, is internationally recognised as a site of importance for migratory birds. Here, you can admire dense mangrove forests, mudflats, ponds, and if you’re lucky, you’ll spot some of the park’s residents like crabs, shellfish, water snakes, monitor lizards, and otters.
MacRitchie Reservoir Park is another tranquil spot. The park’s thriving jungle-like hiking trails will make you instantly forget city life, with leafy walkways and the musical chirping of birds. Find the way that leads you to the treetop walk, a 25-metre high free-standing suspended bridge that offers a bird’s eye view of the forest canopy.
For panoramic vistas of the city, look no further than the Southern Ridges. Linking up 10 kilometres of popular green spaces like Mount Faber Park, Telok Blangah Hill Park, and HortPark with a series of bridges and trails, this is one of Singapore’s most scenic hiking attractions. A highlight here is Henderson Waves, an Instagram-worthy architectural gem known for its distinctive wave-like structure.
Labrador Nature Reserve also offers a little bit of heritage for history lovers, with the remnants of a British military battlement, along with a charming view of the sea.
In the heartlands, there’s Jurong Lake Gardens. Built around Jurong Lake, the Gardens form Singapore’s third national garden, with lush forest trails and a 2.3-hectare space dedicated to 13 play zones for kids. There’s also a waterfront facility where you can try your hand at paddle-boating or kayaking, as well as a man-made island featuring a butterfly-filled flower field.
Insider Tips
  • You can spot plenty of macaques at certain nature reserves. While they are adorable, please do not provoke or feed them. Also, keep your plastic bottles and bags out of sight.
